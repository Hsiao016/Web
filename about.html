About me

I'm a PhD student at the University of Westminster, Contemporary China Centre. I got BA in Chinese Language and Literature from Soochow University and MA in Digital Culture and Communication from the University of Sheffield.

I'm interested in:(1)How industrial heritage making in post-socialist China is part of the politics of memory, how socialist memory is contested by multiple actors and (2)How the heritage making serves a broader post-socialist urban entrepreneurialism. My PhD project is 'The Haunting Socialist Memory: China’s Third Front Industrial Heritage, Public Secrets and Exposure'.

I'm also interested in issues including the "regeneration" of the UK's industrial cities and the impact of international students on them, feminism and queer in post-socialist China, and methodological experiments in the use of digital humanities. In addition, I love photography, attending cultural events and walking in the city. I've lived in Chengdu, Suzhou, Sheffield and Reading, and I now live in London.

My research revolves around the metaphor of the "Ghost"or "Haunting". By viewing those uncomfortable past, which some have declared "dead", as haunting, I conduct my study of industrial heritage, whether in China or the UK. "吊鬼既然早被打死，为什么现在还会引出真的来呢？"
